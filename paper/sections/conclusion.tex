\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
% ============================================================

We presented the first benchmark of amplitude, angle, and basis encoding as
explicit classical feature maps, with spectral attribution of failure modes
across ten datasets and eight classical baselines.
Quantum-inspired encodings achieve no statistically significant accuracy
advantage under matched representational budget (0 wins, 24/30 significantly
worse).
Each encoding's failure is attributable to a distinct geometric property:
$\ell_2$-induced rank collapse, trigonometric-linear equivalence, or binary
Hamming geometry mismatch.
The one structural condition under which near-parity is achievable, namely
uniform filling of the output hypersphere, is an explicit, falsifiable
prediction of the framework.
These findings do not close the question of quantum advantage in machine
learning; they sharpen it by ruling out fixed feature-map geometry as its
source.
